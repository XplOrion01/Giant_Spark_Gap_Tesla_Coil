Secondary Circuit and Top Load (Toroid) Assembly

I. Secondary Coil Physical Specifications

The secondary coil is a high-Q resonant inductor engineered to provide maximum voltage magnification via resonant transformer action.

i. Coil Form: A 1200 mm tall, 80 mm diameter non-conductive PVC pipe.

ii. Winding Length: The enameled wire is precision-wound along $1000\text{ mm}$ (1 meter) of the pipe. The remaining $200\text{ mm}$ at the bottom serves 
as an un-wound structural base that inserts into the main chassis/stand.

iii. Conductor & Turn Count: Wound using 28 SWG (Standard Wire Gauge) enameled copper wire. The coil features approximately 2,500 continuous turns.

iv. Material Quantity: Approximately $630\text{ meters}$ of 28 SWG wire was utilized (from a $700\text{ meter}$ spool).

II. Top Load (Toroid) Design

The top load acts as a capacitor to store energy before it discharges as a spark, and its smooth profile prevents premature breakout.

i. Geometry: A spherical geometry was utilized, constructed from a $200\text{ mm}$ diameter plastic core.

ii. Conductive Layer: The core is completely encapsulated in two layers of heavy-duty aluminum foil to create a smooth, highly conductive surface area.

iii. Placement: The sphere is securely mounted directly atop the secondary PVC coil form.

III. Electrical Interconnections and Grounding Network

Proper grounding is the most critical safety and functional aspect of the secondary circuit. The connections are arranged as follows:

i. High-Potential Termination: The top terminal of the 28 SWG secondary winding is electrically bonded directly to the aluminum foil of the top load.

ii. Low-Potential (Ground) Termination: The base terminal of the secondary winding is routed to a dedicated RF ground. For this setup, it is connected to 
    the building's electrical grounding system via the earth pin of a standard domestic mains socket.

 CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING: The secondary circuit requires a highly reliable, low-resistance earth 
 ground to function properly and safely. Before operation, the mains socket earth connection must be rigorously verified using a multimeter or socket tester. 
 A weak or broken ground will prevent the coil from firing and can cause high-voltage feedback into household appliances or the primary circuit.
