## What is a Spark Gap Tesla Coil (SGTC)?
A Spark Gap Tesla Coil is the classic dual-resonant disruptive discharge circuit originally designed by Nikola Tesla. It relies on a high-voltage transformer to charge a primary capacitor bank until the voltage reaches the breakdown threshold of a physical air gap (the spark gap). 

When the air gap ionizes and fires, it forms a closed resonant circuit, transferring energy inductively from the heavy primary coil to the fine-wound secondary coil. Because the primary and secondary circuits are tuned to the exact same resonant frequency, the voltage multiplies exponentially via resonant transformer action, culminating in high-frequency, high-voltage streamers breaking out from the top load (toroid).

## Critical Safety Warning & Disclaimer
High-voltage projects of this magnitude carry inherent lethal risks. 
* **Mains Isolation:** Always utilize a properly rated two-pole Miniature Circuit Breaker (MCB) and an easily accessible physical emergency stop.
* **RF Grounding:** **Never** operate the secondary coil without verifying a true, low-resistance Earth ground connection. Inadequate grounding will cause immediate high-voltage feedback into the electrical grid.
* **Fire Safety:** Due to extreme thermal loads on the spark gap and ballast system, **always maintain a dedicated fire extinguisher** in the testing perimeter.
* **Ozone & Ventilation:** Ensure the operating environment has active air circulation to disperse ozone and nitrous oxide gases generated by the spark gap arc.
